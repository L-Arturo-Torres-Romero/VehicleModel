\section{Introduction} \label{sec:Introduction}

Autonomous driving and advanced driver assistance systems require reliable lateral control strategies to ensure vehicle stability and accurate path tracking. Among the different control approaches available, state-feedback techniques based on pole placement provide a simple and effective solution for controller design. This work presents an error feedback controller for vehicle lateral dynamics using the Ackermann technique. The proposed implementation is intended as a straightforward reference model that can be used for educational purposes, benchmarking against more advanced controllers, and rapid simulation studies in vehicle dynamics applications.

\section{Lateral Model Symbols} \label{sec:Symbols}

This section describes the symbols used in equations. This is based on the lateral dynamics model described in \cite{rajamani2011vehicle}, that is a linearized model of the error respect the center of the lane of the road. For more details, you can see the lateral dynamics block documentation. The symbols are as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
     \item	\(V\): Velocity of center of gravity of the vehicle (C.G.).
     \item	\(\beta\) : Slip angle. This is the angle that makes V and the longitudinal axis of the vehicle.
     \item	\(\psi\): Heading angle (yaw angle).
     \item	\(\gamma\): \(\psi+ \beta\), this is the angle between V and the X axis of the inertial reference frame, and it is called “the course angle”.
     \item	\(\delta\): Front steer angle.
     \item	\(l_f,l_r\): Distance from C to A, and C to B, respectively.Distances from the C.G. to the front and rear axis of the vehicle.
     \item	\(L= l_f+l_r\)
     \item	X,Y: axis of the inertial frame reference.
     \item	\(C_{\alpha r}, C_{\alpha f}\): Cornering stiffness of rear and front wheels. The cornering stiffness is the ability of the tire to resist deformation while the vehicle is cornering.
     \item	\(I_z\): This is the momentum of inertia of the vehicle respecting the z axis.
     \item	\(V_x\): This is the longitudinal velocity.
\end{enumerate}


\section{Error Feedback (Ackermann) for Lateral Dynamics}
\label{sec:PID4LateralDynamics}

Based on the error model for the lateral dynamics described in \cite{rajamani2011vehicle} and recalling the Ackerman’s formula for the system \(\dot{\textbf{X}} =\textbf{A}\textbf{X}+\textbf{B}u\) where u would be our \(\delta\) and $\textbf{X} $ = $ [x_1\ x_2\   x_3\ x_4] $ = $ [e_1\ \dot{e}_1\ e_2\ \dot{e}_2]$ where the errors are 
\begin{itemize}
  \item $e_1$: lateral error.
  \item $\dot{e}_1$: rate of lateral error.
  \item $e_2$: yaw rate error.
  \item $\dot{e}_2$: rate of yaw rate error.
\end{itemize}
And then 

\begin{equation} \label{eq:MatA}
   \textbf{A} =       \begin{bmatrix}
                      0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
                      
                      0 & 
                      -\frac{2C_{\alpha f}+2C_{\alpha r}}{mV_x}  & 
                      \frac{2C_{\alpha f}+2C_{\alpha r}}{m}        & 
                      \frac{-2C_{\alpha f}l_f+2C_{\alpha r}l_r}{mV_x} \\
                      
                      0 & 0  & 0 & 1 \\
                      
                      
                      0 & 
                      -\frac{2C_{\alpha f}l_f - 2C_{\alpha r}l_r}{I_zV_x} & 
                      \frac{2C_{\alpha f}l_f  - 2C_{\alpha r}l_r}{I_z} & 
                      -\frac{2C_{\alpha f}{l_f}^2 - 2C_{\alpha r}{l_r}^2}{I_zV_x}
                  \end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................
\begin{equation} \label{eq:MatB1}
      \textbf{B}_1 =       \begin{bmatrix}
                          0 \\
                          \frac{2C_{\alpha f}}{m} \\
                          0 \\
                          \frac{2C_{\alpha f}l_f}{I_z} 
                         \end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................  
\begin{equation} \label{eq:MatB2}
      \textbf{B}_2 =       \begin{bmatrix}
                          0 \\
                          -\frac{2C_{\alpha f}l_f -2C_{\alpha r}l_r}{mV_x}-V_x \\
                          0 \\
                          -\frac{2C_{\alpha f}{l_f}^2 - 2C_{\alpha r}{l_r}^2}{I_zV_x}
                          \end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................
\begin{equation} \label{eq:MatB3}
         \textbf{B}_3 =       \begin{bmatrix}
                             0 \\
                             g \\
                             0 \\
                             0 
                             \end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................
where our controller is defined as:
\begin{equation} \label{eq:Uctrl}
u = -\textbf{Kx}
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................
where the gain \textbf{K} is obtained using the Ackermann’s formula as follows:
\begin{equation} \label{eq:Uctrl_}
\textbf{K} = [0 \; 0\; ...\; 0 \; 1][\textbf{B}|\textbf{AB}|...|\textbf{A}^{n-1}\textbf{B}]^{-1}\textbf{H}(\textbf{A})
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................
And we call the controllability matrix \textbf{Mc} to \([\textbf{B}|\textbf{AB}|...|\textbf{A}^{n-1}\textbf{B}]\). Having the poles \{\(p_{d1} \; p_{d2} \; p_{d3}\;\cdot\cdot\cdot\;p_{dn}\)\} and \textbf{I} is the identity matrix:
%.............................................................................................
\begin{equation} \label{eq:HA}
\textbf{H(A)} = (\textbf{A}-p_{d1}\textbf{I})(\textbf{A}-p_{d2}\textbf{I})\cdot\cdot\cdot(\textbf{A}-p_{dn}\textbf{I})
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................

In our case, we make \textbf{B = B1} (ignoring \textbf{B2}, and \textbf{B3} for simplicity) from  (\ref{eq:MatA}) to  (\ref{eq:MatB3}) and the poles for the simulation \(p_d=[-5-3i -5+3i-7 -10]\).
Using Fig. \ref{eq:MatA} and Fig. \ref{eq:MatB1}


\begin{equation} \label{eq:Mc1}
\textbf{Mc} = [\textbf{B}_1 \; \textbf{AB}_1 \; \textbf{A}^2\textbf{B}_1 \; \textbf{A}^3\textbf{B}_1]
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................
\begin{equation} \label{eq:HA1}
\textbf{H(A)} = (\textbf{A}-p_{d1}\textbf{I})(\textbf{A}-p_{d2}\textbf{I})(\textbf{A}-p_{d3}\textbf{I})(\textbf{A}-p_{d4}\textbf{I})
\end{equation}


Since the rank of the matrix \textbf{Mc}  (\ref{eq:Mc1}) is the same as the quantity of poles, then this matrix is invertible or, in other words, our system is controllable. Hence, the control gain \textbf{K}, is obtained in the form:

\begin{equation} \label{eq:K1}
\textbf{K} = [0 \; 0 \; 0 \; 1]\textbf{Mc}^{-1}\textbf{H(A)}
\end{equation}
%.............................................................................................

\section{Error Feedback (Ackermann) Simulink Block}
\label{sec:Simulink}

The implementation of the Ackermann controller is done in a Simulink block, that can be used as a reference for other implementations. The block has the following interface:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Input 1}: \textbf{E}, The state vector \textbf{X} = $[e_1\ \dot{e}_1\ e_2\ \dot{e}_2]$ that contains the lateral error, the rate of lateral error, the yaw rate error, and the rate of yaw rate error, respectively.
  \item  \textbf{Input 2}: \textbf{Vx}, The longitudinal velocity of the vehicle, that is used to compute the matrices \textbf{A} and \textbf{B} of the system.
  \item \textbf{Output}: The control law \textbf{u} as calculated in (\ref{eq:Uctrl}).
\end{itemize}
